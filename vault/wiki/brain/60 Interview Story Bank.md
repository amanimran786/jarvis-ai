### KPI design and measurement judgment

- Situation: Multiple role-prep loops kept identifying KPI design as a key differentiator, and the strongest grounded metrics in the exports are a 15 percent false negative reduction at Meta and a 20 percent accuracy improvement at TikTok.
- Task: Show that the work was not just “improve a metric,” but choose the right metric to protect quality and safety at scale.
- Action: Frame the story around operational judgment: focus on the error type that mattered, track the metric that represented real quality loss, and improve system performance without hiding behind vanity numbers. Use the Meta false negative reduction as the stronger example when under-enforcement or missed harm is the core risk. Use the TikTok accuracy improvement when the emphasis is classifier quality, review consistency, or measurement discipline.
- Result: This becomes a reusable story about choosing and improving the metric that best represented the operational problem, not just reporting whatever number looked good.
- Signals this story supports: metrics fluency, operational judgment, systems thinking, quality leadership.
- Metrics or evidence to preserve: 15 percent false negative reduction at Meta; 20 percent accuracy improvement at TikTok.
- Risks or caveats: do not collapse both metrics into one blended story. Pick the one that better fits the role and keep attribution precise.

### Policy ambiguity and judgment under uncertainty

- Situation: Interview prep for the YouTube Policy Enforcement Manager role surfaced policy ambiguity handling as a real gap, especially around age-appropriateness, regulatory depth, and precision-versus-recall framing.
- Task: Show judgment when the right answer is not obvious and when the tradeoff is between missed harm and over-enforcement rather than between right and wrong.

- Result: This becomes a reusable judgment story for interviews: stay calm, make the tradeoff explicit, explain what would justify escalation, and do not confuse policy uncertainty with random inconsistency.
- Signals this story supports: judgment, escalation maturity, trust and safety reasoning, decision quality under ambiguity.
- Metrics or evidence to preserve: precision-versus-recall framing, age-appropriateness/policy-enforcement prep, and the explicit tradeoff logic.
- Risks or caveats: this is still stronger as a reasoning-and-judgment story than as a single memorable operational incident. If a sharper real example emerges later, replace this with that.
