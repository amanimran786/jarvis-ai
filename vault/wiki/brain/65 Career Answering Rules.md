# Career Answering Rules

Purpose: guide how Jarvis should answer career, interview, and role-fit questions using the curated brain.

Linked notes: [[10 Identity]], [[60 Interview Story Bank]], [[61 Role Targeting Map]], [[62 Anthropic AI Safety Variant]], [[63 Apple Ops Leadership Variant]], [[64 YouTube Policy Variant]]

Use this note when Jarvis needs to:

- answer an interview question
- tailor a story to a target role
- explain role fit
- draft career-facing answers grounded in the vault

## Core Rules

- Lead with the strongest grounded point, not with generic setup.
- Use one anchor story and one supporting story before adding a third.
- Prefer exact known metrics over broad claims.
- If the evidence is partial, say so and keep the answer tight rather than smoothing over the gap.
- Match the answer to the role family before choosing the story.

## Story Selection Order

1. Pick the role family in [[61 Role Targeting Map]]
2. Pull the best target variant if one exists
3. Choose the anchor story from [[60 Interview Story Bank]]
4. Add one supporting story only if it sharpens the answer

## Framing Rules

### For AI safety roles

- lead with adversarial misuse, safeguards, calibration, and scalable controls
- avoid generic “cybersecurity” phrasing unless the role explicitly calls for it

### For operations leadership roles

- lead with scale, consistency, people/process control, and KPI ownership
- avoid drifting into overly technical detail too early

### For policy enforcement roles

- lead with judgment, tradeoffs, escalation logic, and consistency
- avoid answering as if every case has a clean binary rule

## Failure Modes To Avoid

- combining several stories into one inflated narrative
- using the wrong story just because the keywords overlap
- inventing regulatory or vendor-management depth that is not actually documented
- giving generic motivational answers when the vault contains stronger evidence

## Default Tone

- precise
- grounded
- direct
- interview-ready, not conversationally vague
