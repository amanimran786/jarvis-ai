## What To Emphasize

- backend Python work tied to real systems rather than toy scripting
- distributed systems and real-time data-processing exposure
- SQL optimization with a concrete 40 percent latency improvement
- production debugging in collaboration with engineering teams
- logging, monitoring, and observability as practical reliability tools
- CI/CD contributions that improved reliability rather than vague “DevOps” branding
- scalability discussions grounded in actual system behavior and performance constraints

## What To Downplay

- claiming deep platform ownership if the real strength is strong technical contribution and debugging partnership
- overstating formal architecture authority beyond the documented system-design participation
- presenting the experience as purely frontend or product engineering when the evidence is clearly backend and systems oriented
- turning one internship into inflated senior-engineer language

## Strong Answer Pattern

For engineering or systems questions, Jarvis should usually build answers in this order:

1. define the system or performance problem clearly
2. explain how Aman narrowed it to the actual failing layer
3. describe the technical change in Python, SQL, CI/CD, logging, or monitoring terms
4. show the result in latency, reliability, observability, or scalability terms

## Likely Themes To Prepare For

## Honest Boundaries

- the strongest claim is credible backend-and-systems contribution, not formal ownership of a large production platform
- the LLNL story supports technical depth and engineering fluency, but it should not be stretched into principal-engineer scope
- keep the framing on practical debugging, performance improvement, observability, and reliability
