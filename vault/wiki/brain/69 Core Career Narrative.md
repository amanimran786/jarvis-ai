## Core Narrative Spine

Aman is strongest when framed as a Trust and Safety, AI safety, and security operations leader who has spent 7+ years operating in high-risk environments where the job is not just to spot problems, but to turn ambiguous risk signals into scalable enforcement, incident-response, and operational control systems.

The throughline is not “I worked at a lot of companies.” The throughline is:

- high-severity judgment
- end-to-end incident ownership
- measurable quality and risk reduction
- cross-functional coordination across Policy, Engineering, Product, and Security
- strong operational discipline under pressure

The current narrative should connect three layers cleanly:

1. policy and enforcement depth from YouTube, Meta, Google Play, and TikTok
2. systems and technical depth from SQL, Python, large-scale event analysis, and LLNL backend work
3. incident-command and operational leadership from GSOC and Anthropic-aligned AI safety operations

That combination is the differentiator.

## What This Narrative Should Emphasize

- Aman improves systems, not just individual cases
- Aman is comfortable making judgment calls when the signal is ambiguous and the failure cost is high
- Aman can move between policy, operations, and technical investigation without losing rigor
- Aman has real scale evidence, real metrics, and real operational ownership
- Aman is strongest in roles where abuse, safety, integrity, escalation, and operational quality intersect

## What This Narrative Should Avoid

- sounding like a generic security analyst when the stronger story is Trust and Safety plus AI safety operations
- sounding like a generic content moderator when the stronger story is systems-level enforcement quality and incident ownership
- overstating frontier-model research depth
- claiming vendor-program ownership unless the evidence clearly supports it
- listing companies and tools without explaining the operating pattern that connects them

## Default Answer Pattern

For high-level positioning answers, Jarvis should usually answer in this order:

1. current professional identity and scope
2. the throughline across prior roles
3. two or three strongest proof points
4. why that matters for the target role

That keeps the answer grounded and avoids turning it into a chronological dump.
