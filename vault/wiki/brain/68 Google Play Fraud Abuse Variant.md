## Positioning Summary

For Google Play-style roles, Aman should be framed as a fraud-and-abuse investigations operator with strong platform-risk judgment, not just as a general trust-and-safety analyst and not as a pure policy specialist.

The differentiator is the combination of:

- direct experience investigating coordinated fraud and abuse networks on a large platform
- strong root-cause analysis for enforcement gaps and policy breakdowns
- operational rigor around escalations, reporting, and investigation playbooks
- behavioral-pattern analysis that turns messy signals into risk-mitigation strategy
- cross-functional credibility with Product, Engineering, and Policy partners

## Best Story Stack

### Anchor story

The strongest direct proof is the Google Play role itself:

- led investigations into coordinated fraud and abuse networks across a large-scale platform ecosystem
- built reporting frameworks that improved visibility into platform risk and enforcement performance
- performed root-cause analysis on enforcement gaps and policy issues
- managed high-priority escalations and enforcement decisions
- partnered with Product, Engineering, and Policy to improve detection systems
- analyzed behavioral patterns to inform risk-mitigation strategy
- developed and owned operational playbooks that improved investigation efficiency and consistency

Why:

- This is the cleanest resume-backed evidence for platform-integrity, fraud, and abuse work. It already speaks the language of Google Play-style risk operations without needing extra translation.

### Supporting story 1

- [[60 Interview Story Bank#Systems thinking across operations]]

Why:

- It shows that Aman does not treat fraud incidents as isolated casework. He looks for the failing layer in policy, detection, workflow, or escalation design and then fixes the system.

### Supporting story 2

- [[60 Interview Story Bank#KPI design and measurement judgment]]

Why:

- It reinforces that risk work should be measured and improved, not just handled manually. That matters for platform roles where reporting quality and enforcement visibility drive real decisions.

### Supporting story 3

- [[60 Interview Story Bank#TikTok recurrence reduction through root-cause investigation]]

Why:

- It adds measurable evidence that Aman can reduce repeat failure patterns after identifying the underlying cause, which maps well to fraud, abuse, and policy-gap environments.

## What To Emphasize

- coordinated fraud and abuse investigations
- platform integrity and risk visibility
- reporting frameworks and operational dashboards
- enforcement-gap and policy-gap root-cause analysis
- high-priority escalation judgment
- behavioral-pattern analysis and anomaly detection
- cross-functional work with Product, Engineering, and Policy
- operational playbooks that improve consistency and speed without lowering quality

## What To Downplay

- broad security branding if it hides platform-risk and abuse-investigation depth
- generic AI-safety language when the role is clearly about marketplace or ecosystem abuse
- overclaiming formal product ownership when the stronger truth is cross-functional operational influence
- vague “fraud strategy” language that is not tied to investigations, visibility, and enforcement systems

## Strong Answer Pattern

For Google Play-style interviews, Jarvis should usually build answers in this order:

1. define the abuse or fraud pattern clearly
2. explain how Aman would determine whether the issue is isolated or systemic
3. show how the signal maps to enforcement gaps, policy ambiguity, or detection weakness
4. explain the reporting, escalation, or playbook change that reduces future risk

## Likely Themes To Prepare For

- coordinated actor behavior versus one-off abuse
- when an enforcement gap is really a policy, tooling, or workflow problem
- how to escalate high-priority fraud or abuse cases without creating noise
- what makes a reporting framework useful for Product and Engineering partners
- how to identify behavior patterns early enough to mitigate risk before it scales
- balancing fast enforcement with consistent decision quality

## Honest Gaps

- do not overstate direct anti-financial-fraud specialization if the evidence is broader platform abuse and coordinated-risk investigation
- do not imply formal PM ownership; keep the story grounded in investigation, escalation, reporting, and cross-functional influence
- the strongest proof is operational fraud-and-abuse execution, not a standalone risk-strategy title

## Draft One-Sentence Framing

Aman is strongest for Google Play-style fraud and abuse roles when positioned as an investigations operator who can detect coordinated abuse patterns, diagnose enforcement gaps, build reporting and playbook systems, and partner across Product, Engineering, and Policy to reduce platform risk at scale.
