## Positioning Summary

Aman is strongest for OpenAI-style roles when framed as a trust-and-safety and AI safety operator who can handle high-sensitivity abuse and integrity cases while also building scalable workflows, decision systems, and cross-functional feedback loops.

The fit is especially strong where OpenAI cares about:

- content integrity and scaled enforcement
- emerging risk operations
- fraud, impersonation, and account abuse
- incident management and high-sensitivity escalations
- SOPs, vendor/process quality, and operational systems
- cross-functional incident response that bridges policy, engineering, product, and security

## Why This Fits OpenAI

OpenAI's Trust, Safety, and Risk Operations roles sit at the intersection of abuse prevention, scaled enforcement, ambiguous policy interpretation, fraud and platform integrity, and cross-functional operational design. That fits Aman well because the recurring strength in his background is not just enforcement judgment. It is turning messy risk signals into clearer workflows, calibration, escalation logic, and measurable controls across high-stakes systems.

## What To Emphasize

- abuse, fraud, and emerging-risk investigation
- scalable controls and operational systems
- classifier calibration, quality, and measurable improvement
- incident management for high-sensitivity cases
- cross-functional work with policy, product, engineering, legal, or operations partners
- concrete ownership of reporting, audit logging, and post-incident review frameworks

## What To Downplay

- generic security branding if it hides trust-and-safety and integrity depth
- pure research framing if the role is clearly operations and risk execution
- vendor-management claims that are broader than the evidence supports

## Strong Answer Pattern

For OpenAI-style roles, Jarvis should usually build answers in this order:

1. define the abuse, integrity, or risk problem clearly
2. explain how Aman would validate whether it is real, systemic, and high-priority
3. show how the issue maps to workflow, policy, detection, escalation, or access control
4. explain the scalable operational fix and how to measure whether it worked

## Honest Gaps

- do not overstate formal compliance or legal specialization if the evidence is really stronger on operations, risk judgment, and enforcement
- do not present Aman as a frontier-model researcher when the fit is strongest on trust, safety, and risk operations

## Draft One-Sentence Framing

Aman is strongest for OpenAI-style trust and safety roles when positioned as an operator who can investigate abuse and integrity risk, make high-stakes decisions under ambiguity, and turn those decisions into scalable operational systems.
