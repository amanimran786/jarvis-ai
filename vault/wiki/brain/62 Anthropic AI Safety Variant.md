## Positioning Summary

Aman is strongest for Anthropic-style roles when framed as an AI safety operations operator, not as a generic security analyst and not as a pure policy generalist.

The differentiator is the combination of:

- trust and safety enforcement depth across major platforms
- current AI safety incident-response ownership in an Anthropic-aligned environment
- adversarial abuse and misuse reasoning
- classifier calibration and quality judgment
- operational debugging mindset
- comfort translating ambiguous risk signals into scalable controls

## What To Emphasize

- classifier precision and recall as operational safety controls
- adversarial misuse detection, prompt-injection and jailbreak-adjacent reasoning
- end-to-end incident response ownership
- calibration and consistent decision quality under ambiguity
- cross-functional work with policy, engineering, and operations partners
- strong ownership without inflated claims

## What To Downplay

- generic GSOC or physical security framing when the role is clearly about AI and model abuse
- broad “cybersecurity” branding if it obscures trust-and-safety depth
- vendor or workforce-management stories unless the interviewer is explicitly probing scaled operations

## Strong Answer Pattern

For Anthropic-style interviews, Jarvis should usually build answers in this order:

1. identify the risk or signal clearly
2. explain how the wrong layer could be blamed if the system is not examined carefully
3. show how Aman narrows the problem to policy, detection, workflow, or calibration
4. explain the scalable fix and the quality tradeoff

That structure matches the real operating style better than generic STAR storytelling alone.

## Likely Themes To Prepare For

- handling jailbreaks, prompt injection, or adversarial misuse
- when to escalate rather than force a weak enforcement decision
- classifier misses and how to decide whether the problem is labels, policy, or thresholds
- balancing false positives and false negatives when harm is asymmetric
- partnering with policy and engineering without losing operational grounding

## Honest Gaps

- do not overclaim direct frontier-model research depth
- do not imply formal model-training ownership if the real strength is operations and enforcement quality
- keep the story grounded in AI safety operations and safeguards execution rather than trying to sound like a research scientist

## Draft One-Sentence Framing

Aman is a trust and safety and AI safety operations specialist who is strongest at turning ambiguous abuse signals into scalable enforcement, calibration, and detection improvements without losing grounding in real operational constraints.
