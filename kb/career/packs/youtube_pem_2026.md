## SECTION 4: ROLE-SPECIFIC QUESTION GUIDANCE

### "Why YouTube / why this role?"
Primary: B4 (YouTube origin story)
Frame: Full-circle narrative — started here in child safety in 2018, built cross-platform
experience (Meta calibration, TikTok data tooling, Anthropic AI safety), now returning
with more to offer.
Tie to: Mohan's #2 priority (kids/teens = the mandate of this role) and #4 (AI-generated
harm = current Anthropic work). Make explicit that both are covered.
Close with: "I'm not coming back because it's Google — I'm coming back because this is
the problem I've been working toward from multiple directions."

### "Tell me about a time you owned quality / calibration at scale"
Primary: B1 (Meta calibration)
Emphasize: This was not a one-time fix — it was a system redesign. IRR below 80%,
identified the guidance gap, rewrote the decision tree, re-audited, saw the improvement.
Do not skip: The IRR metric, the blind audit mechanism, the 2-week recalibration
intervention. Credibility lives in the mechanism.

### "How would you investigate a spike in removal decisions?"
Framework: Spike Diagnosis Framework (from decision_frameworks.md)
YouTube-specific add: Check if spike is concentrated in children's/family categories
— faster path to advertiser impact and brand safety implications → higher urgency.
Check overturn rate the following week: spike in removals + high overturn = over-enforcement
upstream (calibration failure, not a real content trend).
Anchor to: B2 (TikTok spike) as the lived experience version.

### "False positive vs. false negative — which matters more in age-appropriateness?"
Framework: Precision/Recall Tradeoff Framework
Calibration: In high-harm categories (CSAM, child exploitation) — false negatives matter
more. In borderline age-appropriate content at YouTube scale — false positives matter
because a single threshold decision hits algorithm signals, creator earnings, AND
advertiser confidence simultaneously. At 200B daily Shorts views, systematic
over-enforcement is not a minor cost.
Show: You understand the asymmetry changes based on severity tier, not just the general rule.

### "How do you ensure consistency across vendor reviewers / different cultures or languages?"
Primary: B1 (Meta calibration)
Frame: Consistency comes from clarity of guidance, not trust in individual reviewers.
Reviewers do what the guidance tells them. If decisions are drifting, look at the guidance.
Mechanisms: blind audits against gold standard, IRR tracking as leading indicator, appeal
overturn rate as lagging indicator, targeted recalibration sessions on specific failure modes.
Add: Localization creates calibration risk when guidance wasn't designed for the cultural
context — the fix is at the guidance level, not the reviewer level.

### "How would you work with engineering on classifier accuracy?"
Primary: B3 (Engineering pushback) + B2 (TikTok false positive spike)
Frame: Don't argue on engineering's turf. Show the full stakeholder cost matrix:
user harm exposure, creator false positive burden, advertiser confidence impact.
Make the tradeoff visible to all three stakeholders simultaneously.
YouTube-specific angle: "At YouTube, a classifier miscalibration doesn't just affect
one signal — it hits algorithm distribution, creator monetization, and brand safety."

### "How do you think about AI-generated content threats?"
Primary: B5 (Anthropic novel pattern detection)
YouTube-specific add: YouTube's inauthentic content policy update (July 2025), Mohan's
letter on AI detection difficulty, the fact that AI-generated content targeting children
creates classification challenges existing systems weren't designed for.
Show: You're already working on this problem from the AI safety direction. This role
lets you apply it to age-appropriateness, which is where it matters most for users.

### "Why are you leaving Anthropic / why now?"
Frame: Not leaving — completing an arc. Started in child safety, expanded to cross-platform
quality systems, spent time at the AI safety frontier. This role brings all three together.
The timing is right because AI-generated content risks to children are accelerating —
the problems Aman works on at Anthropic are arriving at YouTube right now.
