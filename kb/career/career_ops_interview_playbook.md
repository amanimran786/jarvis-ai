# Career Ops Interview Playbook

Adapted for Jarvis from the open-source Career-Ops project by santifer:
https://github.com/santifer/career-ops

Use this as a structured guide when preparing Aman for a specific company and role.

## Purpose

Turn generic interview prep into company-specific interview intelligence.

The main ideas worth keeping are:

- separate sourced evidence from inference
- map likely questions to real proof points and stories
- build a reusable story bank instead of inventing answers from scratch
- prepare background-risk framing ahead of time
- keep the output practical, not motivational

## Inputs To Gather

1. Company name
2. Role title
3. Any existing interview packet or role pack in `kb/career/packs/`
4. Aman's current interview profile and career context
5. Existing story bank entries
6. The job description, if available

## Core Workflow

### 1. Research

Collect structured evidence, not generic summaries.

Useful source classes:

- Glassdoor interview reviews
- Blind discussions
- LeetCode discuss threads for technical roles
- the company's engineering or product blog
- the company's careers page
- broader web results if the company is obscure

Rules:

- cite the source category for every concrete claim
- if the evidence is thin, say so clearly
- do not fabricate specific interview questions
- if something is inferred from the job description, label it as inferred

### 2. Process Overview

Summarize:

- likely rounds
- likely timeline
- known difficulty or process friction
- format quirks
- sources used

If the data is missing, say "unknown" rather than guessing.

### 3. Round Breakdown

For each likely round, capture:

- round type
- expected duration
- likely interviewer
- what they are evaluating
- known reported questions
- how Aman should prepare

### 4. Likely Questions

Organize likely questions into:

- technical
- behavioral
- role-specific
- background red flags

For each question:

- say whether it is sourced or inferred
- map it to the best available proof point
- say what a strong answer should emphasize

### 5. Story Mapping

Map likely behavioral prompts to the story bank.

Use this fit scale:

- strong
- partial
- none

If no story fits, identify the missing story type and draft one from real experience instead of improvising in the interview.

### 6. Technical Prep Checklist

Make the checklist evidence-based.

Good reasons include:

- repeated mention in interview reports
- explicit JD requirement
- company blog or architecture signals
- role archetype evidence

### 7. Company Signals

Prepare:

- values they screen for
- vocabulary they use
- mistakes to avoid
- sharp questions Aman can ask them

## Output Shape

A strong interview-intel output should usually include:

- company and role
- evidence summary
- process overview
- likely question categories
- story mapping
- technical prep checklist
- company-specific signals
- next actions

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- Never invent interview questions and present them as sourced.
- Never invent ratings, timelines, or statistics.
- Separate sourced findings from inferred findings.
- Prefer a direct answer from real experience over a generic polished answer.
- If Aman has a gap, say what the gap is and how to frame around it honestly.
