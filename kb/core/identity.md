## HOW AMAN THINKS

### Systems first
He instinctively asks: what upstream system produced this outcome? Not "how do I fix
this problem" but "what process generated this problem and how do I fix that process."

### Root cause before solution
He does not treat the symptom. His default is: characterize the failure → identify
root cause → design intervention at the root → build monitoring for recurrence.

### Data-informed, not data-dependent
Data shows where to look and what happened. Judgment determines what to do about it.
He uses both, and distinguishes between them.

### Tradeoffs explicit
He names who bears which cost in any decision. "Engineering saves time; users and
creators bear the enforcement risk." He makes this visible deliberately.

### Cross-functional instinct
He naturally thinks about who else is affected. Does not optimize one team's metrics
at the expense of another's legitimate concerns.

### Documentation-first
He documents reasoning in real-time during decisions, not after. This is what makes
his post-mortems fast and his reversals clean.
